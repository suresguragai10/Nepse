How the signals work

Each stock is scored against the conditions you enable in the Screener tab. SMA: price above its moving average (uptrend). Bollinger Bands: price reclaiming the lower band / not overbought at the upper band. MACD: histogram turning positive (momentum shift up). Avg Volume: today's volume above its average (conviction). Fibonacci Pivot: price above the daily pivot level. Trend: today closed higher than yesterday. When the number of met conditions reaches your threshold, the stock shows BUY.

Divergence (RSI)

Divergence is when price and momentum (RSI) disagree — an early hint that a trend may be tiring. ▲ Bullish divergence: price makes a lower low but RSI makes a higher low — selling pressure is easing, a bounce may be near. ▼ Bearish divergence: price makes a higher high but RSI makes a lower high — the rally is losing steam. The dots (●–●●●) show how strong the gap is. Use the Divergence column and the "Divergence" filter in the Buy Screener to find these setups. Important: divergence is a watch signal, not an automatic buy or sell. It works best at the end of a clear, extended trend and is unreliable in choppy or thin trading — and NEPSE has plenty of both. Always confirm with volume, price action, and the floorsheet before acting. Needs ~40+ days of history to compute, so newly tracked stocks show "—". Educational only, not advice.
